I served as a Graduate Assistant Lecturer and Instructor of Record (IOR) for multiple sections of this upper‑division writing‑intensive course within the Bachelor of Science in Public Health (BSPH) curriculum.

### Semesters Taught
- Spring 2023 (Section 903)  
- Spring 2024 (Section 902)  
- Spring 2025 (Section 902)

### Course Purpose
A writing‑ and reflection‑focused course designed to help students understand how narrative, storytelling, and lived experience shape public health communication, advocacy, and community engagement.

### Course Overview
This course explores narrative as a tool for understanding health behaviors, communicating risk, and advancing public health practice. Students analyze personal, community, and policy narratives; practice reflective writing; and develop skills in crafting compelling, evidence‑informed public health stories for diverse audiences.

### Teaching Responsibilities
- Designed weekly modules integrating narrative theory and public health practice  
- Facilitated reflective writing workshops and peer‑review sessions  
- Provided individualized feedback on narrative essays and final projects  
- Guided students in applying narrative frameworks to health communication  
- Evaluated student work using structured rubrics
