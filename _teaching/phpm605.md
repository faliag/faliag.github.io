I served as a Graduate Assistant Lecturer and Instructor of Record (IOR) for this core Master of Public Health (MPH) course within the Department of Health Policy & Management.

### Semester Taught
- Spring 2026 (Section 600)

### Course Purpose
A foundational MPH core course introducing students to the structure, financing, and governance of the U.S. healthcare system, with emphasis on policy processes, organizational behavior, and managerial decision‑making.

### Course Overview
This course provides an overview of health policy and management principles, including healthcare delivery systems, insurance and financing mechanisms, regulatory frameworks, and policy analysis tools. Students learn to evaluate policy alternatives, understand organizational dynamics, and apply management concepts to real‑world public health challenges.

### Teaching Responsibilities
- Delivered weekly lectures and facilitated graduate‑level discussions  
- Developed case‑based learning modules and policy analysis exercises  
- Provided detailed feedback on policy briefs and written assignments  
- Guided students in applying policy frameworks to contemporary health issues  
- Assessed student performance using rubric‑based evaluation
