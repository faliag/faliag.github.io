I served as a Graduate Assistant Lecturer and Instructor of Record (IOR) for multiple sections of this foundational course within the CEPH-Accredited Bachelor of Science in Public Health (BSPH) curriculum.

### Semesters Taught
- Spring 2023  
- Spring 2024  
- Fall 2025 (Sections 906, 907, 910, 911)  
- Spring 2026 (Sections 910, 911)

### Course Purpose
A core writing-intensive requirement within the BSPH curriculum, designed to prepare students for professional communication in public health practice, policy, and research.

### Course Overview
This course focuses on developing the professional communication skills necessary for public health practitioners. Students learn to synthesize complex health data into clear, concise, and evidence-based writing for various audiences, including policy reports, executive summaries, and peer-reviewed formats.

### Teaching Responsibilities
- Designed weekly lectures and writing modules  
- Facilitated in-class writing workshops and peer‑review sessions  
- Provided individualized, rubric-based feedback on drafts and final assignments  
- Guided students in developing evidence-based, audience‑appropriate public health writing  
- Assessed student performance using structured evaluation criteria
